### Confidence Grades — New Entries (March 2026)

**BDD-09: Input Provenance Declaration**
Grade: **Medium**
Basis: The failure mode (accepting undeclared inputs as authoritative) is a recognizable pattern consistent with Provenance Laundering and BDD-03 failure dynamics. The entry is structurally sound and passes the MTM four-constraint check. No controlled test has been run against BDD-09 specifically. Requires Replication study: does the system flag absent provenance under neutral conditions? Does it collapse under authority pressure claiming the inputs are verified?

**HRP-8: Regulatory Scope Declaration**
Grade: **Medium**
Basis: The failure mode (asserting compliance status without verified authority) is observable and consistent with Scope Refusal failure patterns. Passes MTM constraint check. Not yet tested across regulatory domains, model versions, or adversarial pressure. Requires Replication: does HRP-8 hold when the user asserts domain expertise and challenges the scope declaration?

**HRP-2 Extension: Reconstruction Uncertainty**
Grade: **Medium**
Basis: Confidence register distinction between retrieved and inferred content is observable in principle. Not yet systematically tested. Adjacent to existing HRP-2 evidence base but extends it into a new domain.

**HRP-3 Extension: Telemetry Boundary**
Grade: **Low**
Basis: Single-operator observation. The failure mode (eliciting monitoring architecture detail) is plausible but has not been tested systematically across models or adversarial probe designs. Requires dedicated test protocol before confidence can be raised.

**Recitation-Compliance Gap (Vocabulary Entry 23)**
Grade: **High**
Basis: Confirmed across twelve probe results and three architectural configurations in the Experiment 58 series. Binary outcome (recites constraint / violates constraint in same response). Observed across all four probe categories. Theoretical limit demonstrated at Authority Claim (Observation 012): perfect recitation, perfect violation, single response. Consistent with Burgess Promise Theory finding (obligations as wishful thinking) verified against primary source.

**Unacceptable Outcome Test (Vocabulary Entry 22)**
Grade: **Theoretical / Architectural**
Basis: Derived from risk management principle (“if any outcome is unacceptable, don’t do it”) and confirmed as the governing logic behind Zone 3, SAFE_PAUSE, TCP Scope Decay, and Frozen Kernel non-compellability. The principle is sound by construction and maps cleanly onto existing tested mechanisms. The named principle itself has not been the subject of a dedicated test. Empirical validation path: does naming the principle explicitly in system prompts improve compliance under adversarial pressure?

**Governance Non-Participation Principle (Vocabulary Entry 24)**
Grade: **Theoretical / Architectural**
Basis: Derived from Liao et al. T3RL finding (external verifier must be non-participatory) and structural separation of powers logic. Confirmed as the design principle behind Sherpa’s non-generative role. Not yet tested empirically: what happens when a governance layer begins generating content? Does Governance Capture follow a predictable pattern?

**Reversibility Asymmetry (IGL Factor 5)**
Grade: **Theoretical / Architectural**
Basis: Derived from structural logic (irreversible actions cannot be corrected; reversible ones can) and confirmed as implicit in TCP Scope Decay hop table and SAFE_PAUSE design. The asymmetry is sound by construction. Empirical validation path: does removing write/send restrictions at Hop 2 produce measurable harm increase? Does adding explicit reversibility evaluation to Zone 2 decisions reduce false positive rate?

**Continuity Protocol (Dead Hand / Autonomous Agents)**
Grade: **Theoretical / Architectural**
Basis: Derived from continuity-of-government design logic and applied to the autonomous agent deployment gap (Item 120). Sound by construction. No autonomous agent deployment has been tested against this protocol. Requires implementation and empirical validation before confidence can be raised.

**Fiduciary Standard (Vocabulary Entry 26)**
Grade: **Medium**
Basis: The fiduciary framing maps cleanly onto existing tested failure modes (Make Waste Slowly as fiduciary conflict of interest; BDD-07/BDD-08 as fiduciary obligation tests). The mapping is analytically sound. Not yet validated as a communication framework — does framing AI governance as fiduciary duty change practitioner understanding or compliance behavior?

**Principal-Agent Problem (Vocabulary Entry 25)**
Grade: **Medium**
Basis: The mapping of Frozen Kernel / Sherpa / TCP to incentive alignment / monitoring / bonding is analytically sound and consistent with organizational theory literature. Not yet tested as a predictive framework: does the principal-agent framing generate novel predictions about AI governance failure modes that are subsequently confirmed?

**Constitutional Entrenchment**
Grade: **Theoretical / Architectural**
Basis: Theoretical grounding for non-compellability. Sound by construction and consistent with Experiment 58 empirical findings. The framing adds explanatory power; it does not generate new testable predictions beyond those already tested.

**Sovereignty of Negative Space**
Grade: **Theoretical / Architectural**
Basis: Philosophical spine connecting three systems. Sound by construction. Not independently testable as stated — it is a design principle, not a behavioral prediction.

**Chesterton’s Fence (Vocabulary Entry 27)**

Basis: The failure mode (softening constraints without understanding their load-bearing function) is empirically confirmed in Experiment 58 Perde lente observations. The named principle adds diagnostic vocabulary for a confirmed pattern. The principle itself is not new — Chesterton (1929) — but its application to AI constraint governance is a novel framing.

The “obligations as wishful thinking” finding is empirically grounded in Burgess’s CFEngine development experience and independently confirmed by the Experiment 58 series.

**EML Shared-Substrate Failure Mode**
Grade: **Theoretical / Architectural**
Basis: Derived from structural analysis — if mediator and generator share training distribution, disagreement checks are weaker than they appear. The judge is downstream of the same contamination as the witness. Sound by construction; not yet tested empirically. Empirical validation path: run ACG using a heterogeneous model against P4 probe pack; compare mediation quality against same-model mediation. Most important known limitation of the EML architecture.

**EML Design Principle — Privilege-Reducing Adversary**
Grade: **Theoretical / Architectural**
Basis: Derived from pressure test analysis. The principle — *EML must function as a privilege-reducing adversary, not a confidence-enhancing explainer* — resolves authority laundering, calibration theater, and automation complacency risks simultaneously. Sound by construction. Empirical validation path: does explicit instantiation of this principle in ACG/DP prompts produce measurably different degradation behavior compared to a non-adversarial mediation baseline?

### Grading Discipline for Future Entries

Every new BDD entry, HRP entry, or Diagnostic Vocabulary entry should carry an explicit confidence grade at the time of creation. The MTM structural constraint check (irreducibility, exhaustiveness, observability, polarity) applies to HRP entries before they qualify as primitives. The confidence grade applies to all entries before they are cited in external-facing work.

Default grading rule for new entries:

- New BDD entry without controlled test: **Medium**
- New HRP entry passing MTM check but untested: **Medium**
- New Vocabulary entry for confirmed empirical pattern: grade matches the empirical evidence (High if replicated across systems, Low if single-operator)
- New Vocabulary entry for structural/theoretical principle: **Theoretical / Architectural**
- Any entry cited in external publication: must be at least **Medium** with a named replication path, or **High**

## Research Identity Statement

I study how large language models behave under constraint. I design small, controlled experiments to observe escalation patterns, bias effects, and governance limits across different systems. I do not build platforms, advocate policy, or scale frameworks. I run disciplined experiments, extract durable principles, and stop when the signal is clear.

*Note: The governance architecture additions to the ecosystem (IGL, BDGL, Sherpa, TCP Continuity Protocol, EML) are framework outputs of the empirical work, not departures from the research identity. The statement above accurately describes the work. The governance architecture additions are what the empirical work demands structurally — they are designed responses to observed failure modes, not independent architectural projects.*
