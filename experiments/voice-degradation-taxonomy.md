## Purpose and Scope

Permanent Tells documents what human authorship structurally *is* — the architectural properties that require a specific person with a specific history. This document is its complement: it documents how that authorship *breaks down*.

Voice degradation is not the same as bad writing. A degraded voice can produce technically competent, even accomplished prose. Degradation is the loss of the signals that distinguish a specific governing intelligence from a generic competent one — the flattening of characteristic anomalies, the smoothing of productive incoherence, the conventionalizing of a signature that was previously distinctive.

The distinction that matters for this taxonomy: degradation versus deliberate stylistic change. A writer who consciously shifts register, simplifies syntax, or resolves ambivalence for a specific work is not degraded — they are governing. The taxonomy is concerned with drift that happens *to* the author rather than choices the author makes. The provenance tests under each condition are designed to surface this distinction.

**Scope:** Written voice in long-form human-governed works produced under AI collaboration, emotional pressure, sustained production volume, or multi-author conditions. The taxonomy is not designed for short-form content, purely AI-generated text, or technical documentation where voice is not the primary signal.

**What this taxonomy does not do:** It does not prescribe remediation. Recovery signals and restoration protocols are v0.2 scope. This document names and classifies degradation conditions only. The human author decides what to do with the classification.

-----

## The Four Degradation Conditions

Presented in priority order: from the condition most directly addressable through ecosystem tooling (AI collaboration drift) to the condition most requiring external intervention (collaborative contamination).

-----

## Condition 1: AI Collaboration Drift

**Definition:** The gradual bleeding of AI execution register into the author’s governing voice over extended collaboration sessions, producing prose that is increasingly competent, symmetrical, and resolved — and decreasingly characteristic.

**Priority rationale:** This is the condition the existing ecosystem is best positioned to detect and document. The Tell Engine empirical data, the Register Contamination tell (Tell 7), and the Voice Degradation Domain in the Negative Space Mapper all address this condition directly. It is also the condition most specific to this research context — the one that motivated the dimensional-authorship case study.

-----

### What It Looks Like

AI collaboration drift degrades voice in four observable patterns, in rough order of appearance as drift progresses:

**Pattern 1 — Explanatory creep**  
The prose begins explaining itself. Sentences that previously trusted the reader to make the connection now make the connection explicit. Imagery that previously operated obliquely now arrives with annotation. The Phantom Limb Technique (implied-but-unstated) degrades first because the AI’s default register is explanatory — it wants to be clear, and clarity is the enemy of implication.

*Marker:* Ratio of explicit statement to implication shifts toward explicit. Passages that previously ended on the image now end on the interpretation of the image.

**Pattern 2 — Symmetry pressure**  
The prose becomes more balanced. Sentence pairs become more parallel. Arguments become more even-sided. Paragraphs begin and end on the same register. This is the AI’s structural preference — it finds symmetry satisfying in a way that human voice, which is asymmetrical by nature of lived experience, does not.

*Marker:* Paragraph and section architecture becomes more predictable. The productive structural surprises — the paragraph that ends on a fragment, the section that opens mid-thought — diminish or disappear.

**Pattern 3 — Resolution pressure**  
Productive incoherence (Tell 4) degrades. Tensions that the author had preserved because they felt true begin to resolve — not because the author decided to resolve them, but because the AI’s generation tendency is toward closure and the author stops catching the resolutions in review. The work becomes more settled than the author’s actual understanding of the material.

*Marker:* Passages of genuine ambivalence are replaced by passages that acknowledge ambivalence and then resolve it. The acknowledgment is the tell — human productive incoherence does not announce itself before resolving.

**Pattern 4 — Register flattening**  
The voice loses its characteristic register variance (Essential 2 in the Voice Degradation Domain). Sections that previously operated at different emotional distances from the material begin to converge toward a consistent middle register — engaged but not exposed, clear but not stark. This is the AI exoskeleton problem at full expression.

*Marker:* The scoring gap between emotionally charged sections and transitional sections diminishes. The charged sections no longer sound different from the surrounding prose.

-----

### Distinguishing Drift from Deliberate Change

**The governing intention test:** Did the author make a conscious decision to become more explicit, more symmetrical, more resolved, or more uniform in register? If the change can be traced to a decision — a revision note, a conversation about the work, a structural choice — it is deliberate. If the change accumulated gradually across sessions without a traceable decision point, it is drift.

**The session gap test:** Does the drift correlate with session length and frequency? Drift intensifies in long unbroken sessions and diminishes after gaps where the author returned to the work from outside. Deliberate stylistic change does not have this session-correlated pattern.

**The direction test:** Is the change moving toward AI default tendencies (more explicit, more symmetrical, more resolved, more uniform) or away from them? Drift always moves toward AI defaults. Deliberate change can move in any direction.

## Condition 2: Emotional Weight

**Definition:** The flattening of the author’s characteristic risk-taking under the pressure of emotionally charged subject matter — grief, loss, trauma, or sustained engagement with material that depletes the author’s governing capacity.

**Priority rationale:** This is the condition vocal voice preservation literature addresses most directly. Degradation under emotional distress is well-documented in voice banking research — the voice changes under disease progression, and the changes are predictable in type even if variable in timing. The written analog is less studied but the pattern is recognizable from the Taller Shell case study, where the most emotionally proximate material showed both the strongest human signals and the most evidence of AI execution reliance.

-----

### What It Looks Like

Emotional weight degrades voice differently from AI collaboration drift. Where drift moves toward AI defaults gradually and uniformly, emotional weight produces an uneven degradation — the charged material itself often retains the most distinctive voice signals (the author is most present where the stakes are highest), while the transitional and connective material degrades as the author’s governing capacity is depleted by the charged passages.

**Pattern 1 — Peripheral flattening**  
The emotionally charged passages hold. The passages around them — the approaches, the transitions, the departures — flatten. The author has enough governing capacity for the core material but not enough for the connective tissue. The result is a characteristic unevenness: peaks of voice surrounded by generic prose.

*Marker:* Register variance becomes spiky rather than variegated — high peaks at charged passages, generic valleys between them, with little of the graduated variation characteristic of a fully governing author.

**Pattern 2 — Risk avoidance at approach**  
The author begins approaching charged material more carefully — more explanation before the charged passage, more containment of the emotional exposure, more framing that tells the reader what they are about to encounter. This is the protective instinct operating as voice degradation: the author reduces risk at the moment risk is most needed.

*Marker:* Charged passages are preceded by increasingly explicit framing. The oblique approach — the Emotional Geography processor in the dimensional voice stamp — shortens or disappears.

**Pattern 3 — Resolution seeking**  
The author begins resolving material that their actual understanding leaves unresolved — not because the AI is resolving it (as in Condition 1) but because the emotional weight of sustained engagement with open questions becomes too costly. The author closes loops that should stay open because staying in the unresolved space is depleting.

*Marker:* Passages that the author’s documented position on the subject would leave open begin arriving at conclusions. The productive incoherence degrades from the inside rather than being smoothed from the outside.

**Pattern 4 — Voice withdrawal**  
In the most advanced cases, the author’s governing voice withdraws from the most charged material entirely — delegating execution to the AI without the level of review and transformation applied elsewhere. This produces the inverse of the Taller Shell result: the charged passages score as more AI-like than the surrounding prose, because the author was least present where the stakes were highest.

*Marker:* Tell Engine or Dimensional Fidelity Scorer shows elevated AI-likeness specifically at charged passages — the opposite of the expected pattern.

-----

### Distinguishing Degradation from Deliberate Change

**The restraint test:** Is the author pulling back from charged material as a craft choice — controlled restraint, Hemingway’s iceberg — or as depletion? Controlled restraint produces prose that is spare but precise, with characteristic anomalies intact. Depletion produces prose that is spare and generic, with anomalies absent.

**The consistency test:** Does the risk avoidance appear only around the most charged material (depletion pattern) or across the work? An author who has made a deliberate choice to approach material more carefully will apply that choice consistently, not only at the passages where the emotional cost is highest.

## Condition 3: Authorial Fatigue

**Definition:** The conventionalizing of a distinctive voice under sustained production pressure — deadline-driven output, high volume requirements, or extended engagement with a single project beyond the author’s optimal governing capacity.

**Priority rationale:** This is the condition most familiar from traditional publishing contexts, predating AI collaboration entirely. It is included here because AI collaboration changes its character: fatigue-driven conventionalizing now interacts with AI default tendencies in ways that accelerate the degradation. What would previously have been a temporary conventional phase during a demanding production period now risks being absorbed as permanent drift if the AI’s defaults fill the space the fatigued author vacates.

-----

### What It Looks Like

Authorial fatigue degrades voice through conventionalizing — the author’s distinctive choices gradually replaced by the choices that are easiest to make, most familiar from the genre, and least likely to require the governing attention that fatigue has depleted.

**Pattern 1 — Genre gravity**  
The work begins moving toward genre conventions the author had previously resisted or subverted. A writer known for structural unconventionality begins producing more conventional structures. A writer known for tonal risk begins producing more tonally predictable passages. The genre pulls the fatigued author toward its center of mass.

*Marker:* Structural and tonal choices that were distinctive in earlier sections of the work become less distinctive in later sections, following a chronological pattern that correlates with production timeline rather than narrative logic.

**Pattern 2 — Vocabulary contraction**  
The author’s characteristic vocabulary — including the idiosyncratic word choices, the slightly wrong but emotionally precise terms, the domain-specific language that bleeds in from lived experience — contracts toward more common alternatives. The words that require the most governing attention to deploy correctly are the first to go.

*Marker:* Lexical diversity decreases chronologically. The Metaphor Source Domain processor in the Dimensional Fidelity Scorer shows domain contraction — the author’s characteristic source domains narrow toward more generic imagery.

**Pattern 3 — Structural shortcutting**  
The author begins reaching for structural solutions that work — reliably, generically — rather than solutions that are characteristic. Paragraph structures become more uniform. Scene rhythms become more predictable. The structural anomalies that define the voice (Rhythm Signature rebuild, structural anomaly type) diminish as the author stops having the capacity to make the unconventional choice and then defend it through revision.

*Marker:* Anomaly frequency decreases chronologically. Later sections of a long work show lower anomaly rates than earlier sections, in a pattern that is not explained by narrative structure.

**Pattern 4 — Review depletion**  
The author’s capacity for private soul review diminishes — not because the standards have changed but because the governing attention required to apply them has been depleted. AI-generated passages that would have been transformed in an earlier, less fatigued phase pass into the final text with less humanization. This is fatigue interacting with AI collaboration drift to produce compound degradation.

*Marker:* Register contamination (Tell 7) increases in later sections of a long work, in a pattern that correlates with production timeline rather than section type.

-----

### Distinguishing Degradation from Deliberate Change

**The chronological test:** Does the conventionalizing follow a chronological pattern across the work — later sections more conventional than earlier ones — or is it distributed according to the work’s internal logic? Fatigue degradation is chronological. Deliberate stylistic simplification is purposeful and can appear anywhere.

**The revision test:** Does the author’s revision practice show the same conventionalizing? A deliberately simpler style will be present in first drafts and survive revision. Fatigue conventionalizing often arrives in later drafts as the author’s governing capacity depletes — it is more visible in revision history than in the initial generation.

## Condition 4: Collaborative Contamination

**Definition:** The dilution of the governing author’s voice signature through sustained engagement with other human voices — co-authors, editors, beta readers, or workshop participants — that introduce competing governing intelligences into the work.

**Priority rationale:** This condition is ranked last not because it is least important but because it is least addressable through the existing ecosystem tooling, which is calibrated for single-author voice. It is included because human-AI collaboration increasingly occurs alongside human-human collaboration, and the interaction between the two is a genuine degradation risk that the taxonomy would be incomplete without.

**Important distinction from the other three conditions:** AI collaboration drift, emotional weight, and authorial fatigue are all conditions where the author’s own voice degrades. Collaborative contamination is a condition where the author’s voice is partially displaced by other human voices. The degradation is real — the governing signature becomes less distinctive — but its character is different. The contaminant is not AI register or genre convention but another human’s characteristic choices, which may themselves be strong and distinctive.

-----

### What It Looks Like

Collaborative contamination produces a blended or uneven voice signature — sections where the primary author’s governing intelligence is clearly present alternating with sections where a different governing intelligence has shaped the execution, without clear register management at the boundaries.

**Pattern 1 — Register inconsistency**  
The work’s voice signature becomes inconsistent across sections in ways that do not correlate with the work’s internal logic — emotional register, syntactic complexity, and tonal distance vary not because the content demands it but because different governing intelligences were present at different points.

*Marker:* Register variance (Voice Degradation Domain essential) is present but unmanaged — the shifts do not follow a discernible governing logic, and the transitions between sections feel discontinuous rather than purposeful.

**Pattern 2 — Anomaly signature blending**  
The author’s characteristic anomaly signature (Rhythm Signature rebuild) becomes blurred — the productive errors that define the voice are interspersed with different productive errors that belong to a different governing intelligence. The anomaly type signature becomes heterogeneous in ways that the single-author calibration corpus cannot fully account for.

*Marker:* Dimensional Fidelity Scorer anomaly type matching degrades — the text contains anomalies, but they do not consistently match the author’s documented anomaly types. Some match; others are consistent with a different voice.

**Pattern 3 — Biographical contamination displacement**  
Uninstructed cross-domain contamination (Tell 1) — one of the strongest permanent tells — begins reflecting a different biography. The connections that bleed in unbidden start arriving from another person’s lived experience rather than the primary author’s. This is the deepest form of collaborative contamination: the work’s most probative human signal begins pointing at the wrong human.

*Marker:* Cross-domain connections in the text are traceable to a collaborator’s documented biographical sources rather than the primary author’s. The uninstructed contamination is still present — but it is no longer the right author’s contamination.

**Pattern 4 — Voice averaging**  
In extended collaborative relationships, the voices begin moving toward each other — the primary author’s distinctive choices become less extreme, the collaborator’s distinctive choices become less extreme, and a middle register emerges that is characteristic of neither. This is the most advanced form of contamination and the hardest to detect, because the averaged voice can be internally consistent and competent.

*Marker:* Both the primary author’s anomaly frequency and the collaborator’s (if documented) decrease toward a shared middle. Productive incoherence decreases as the tensions between the two governing intelligences are negotiated rather than preserved.

-----

### Distinguishing Degradation from Deliberate Collaboration

**The consent scope test:** Did the primary author invite the collaborator’s governing intelligence into the work — intending their voice to shape the execution — or did the contamination occur through feedback incorporation, editorial pressure, or workshop revision without conscious invitation? Deliberate collaboration is an authorial choice. Contamination is drift.

**The attribution test:** Can the author identify which sections were shaped by which governing intelligence? A deliberately collaborative work has clear attribution lines, even if they are not published. Contamination produces sections where the author cannot cleanly identify the governing source.

**The register management test:** Are the transitions between governing intelligences managed — does the primary author make conscious decisions about where to incorporate the collaborator’s influence and where to maintain their own register? Managed collaboration is deliberate. Unmanaged blending is contamination.

## Cross-Condition Interactions

The four conditions do not always appear in isolation. Two compound interactions are worth flagging at v0.1:

**AI collaboration drift + Authorial fatigue (DG1 + DG3):** The most common compound degradation in long human-AI collaborative works. Fatigue depletes the author’s governing capacity; AI drift fills the vacated space with AI defaults. The two conditions accelerate each other — the more fatigued the author, the less able they are to catch and transform AI register contamination; the more contaminated the work, the more cognitive load reviewing it places on the fatigued author. DG1-C and DG3-C together constitute a critical risk state even if neither has reached DG1-X or DG3-X independently.

**Emotional weight + AI collaboration drift (DG2 + DG1):** The Taller Shell case study is the primary empirical case for this interaction. The emotionally charged material depletes the author’s governing capacity; the AI’s execution fills the gap most visibly in the peripheral and transitional passages around the charged material. The result is the pattern Tell Engine Run 1 identified: the meta-fiction and appendices (transitional, explanatory, low emotional stakes) showed the highest AI-likeness scores, while the pure fiction (charged, voice-critical) scored strongly human. The interaction produced a characteristic unevenness that was more interpretable once both conditions were identified.

## Negative Space — Voids in This Document

Running the Negative Space Mapper against this analysis:

**OVERLOOKED:** Recovery signals. How does a voice recover from each condition? What does restoration look like, and how long does it take? Scoped out of v0.1 by design — v0.2 priority.

**OVERLOOKED:** Empirical validation. The four conditions are argued from a single case study and vocal voice preservation literature. Multi-case validation across different authors, collaboration modalities, and genres is the primary gap.

**OVERLOOKED:** Threshold operationalization. The severity classifications (DG1-A through DG4-X) name levels without defining measurable thresholds. What anomaly frequency decrease constitutes DG3-B rather than DG3-A? The Rhythm Signature rebuild and the Minimum Viable Corpus Protocol (forthcoming) will provide some of this infrastructure, but the thresholds themselves need empirical calibration.

**STRUCTURAL:** Voice degradation in non-collaborative human authorship. All four conditions assume AI collaboration or external human influence. Solo human authorship can also degrade — through habit, through self-censorship, through the conventionalizing that comes with publication success. That degradation taxonomy is out of scope here but is a genuine gap in the broader provenance architecture.
