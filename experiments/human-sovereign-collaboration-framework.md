## What This Is

A framework for certifying that a creative or intellectual work was produced under **human sovereign governance** — not merely that a human was involved, but that a human was *in command* of the decisions that define the work.

This is not a detector. It does not test whether a work is AI-generated or human-generated. That question is increasingly unanswerable from the artifact alone, and the provenance-problem-map has documented why the window for artifact-level detection is closing.

This framework answers a different question: **What collaboration modality was operating when this work was made?** It certifies a state, not an absence.

-----

## Why Now

Three converging forces make this framework urgent.

**The closing window.** Current AI-generated content contains involuntary tells — stylometric patterns, prosodic artifacts, behavioral signatures that trained observers can recognize as synthetic. These tells are doing real safety work. They create friction. Friction creates doubt. Doubt prompts verification. That friction is temporary. The stylometric window for casual text detection is estimated at 4–6 months; professional detection follows within years. When the window closes, the involuntary safety mechanism disappears (Human Provenance Problem Map, March 2026).

**The inverted heuristic.** High-quality human-AI collaboration is *already* being misread as synthetic, while AI-generated content with human surface signals passes undetected. The Sovereignty Paradox documents the mechanism: detectors were calibrated on solo human production, which had characteristic execution-capacity artifacts — dead spots, uneven drafting, scar tissue. Effective collaboration eliminates those artifacts while preserving human governing intelligence. The heuristic now penalizes success. (Sovereignty Paradox, AI Collaboration Field Guide)

**The modality question.** The right question was never “did AI touch this?” — AI assistance is now ubiquitous. The right question is *who was governing*. The Taller Shell Trilogy case study demonstrates this empirically: when run through the Tell Engine (v2.1), the collaboration scored in the strongly human-leaning range across every substantive dimension. The single flag — “over-cleanliness” — was correctly identified as evidence of effective collaboration, not synthetic authorship. The detector named the collaboration modality. This framework makes that naming formal and verifiable.

## The Discipline

This document takes the same discipline as the Human Provenance Problem Map: **no architecture before the problem is understood.**

Framework Fabrication Syndrome — building elaborate systems before the problem space is characterized — is one of the failure modes this ecosystem exists to prevent. The framework is presented here as a characterization of what needs to be certified and why, followed by the minimum viable attestation structure that makes certification possible.

What this framework does not specify: technical implementation details, cryptographic specifics, or enforcement mechanisms. Those are architecture questions that come after framework adoption.

## What Is Being Certified

The HSCF does not certify that no AI was used. It certifies the presence of five properties, each of which is observable, documentable, and structurally distinct from anything AI can generate without a human governing it.

These five properties are derived empirically from the Silicon Symphony behavioral studies, the Taller Shell case study, and the Sovereignty Paradox framework convergence:

### Property 1: Human Center of Gravity

The work has a stable moral, aesthetic, and interpretive core that **predates and survives the AI’s involvement**.

A human center of gravity means: remove the AI, and the work is smaller — but the same work. The themes, the argument, the emotional truth are intact. The AI added range. The human supplied center.

*What makes this certifiable:* The ROSETTA voice baseline — the pre-collaboration voice sample that establishes what the author sounded like before AI assistance began. Center of gravity is demonstrated by showing that the work’s governing concerns trace to an established human voice, not to prompt engineering.

*What AI cannot fake retroactively:* A center of gravity requires a person who existed before the work began, with values, biography, and preoccupations. This cannot be manufactured after the fact.

-----

### Property 2: AI as Force Multiplier

The AI increased output, memory, iteration speed, and option range. **It did not supply the governing intelligence.**

The distinction: a force multiplier makes the human more capable. A replacement makes the human unnecessary. Both may produce similar artifacts. Only one constitutes human authorship.

*What makes this certifiable:* The decision record — specifically, the pattern of AI-generated options that were *rejected*. A human using AI as a force multiplier generates rejection events. An AI operating without sovereign human governance generates no authentic rejections — the output is the decision.

*What AI cannot fake retroactively:* The record of what the AI proposed and the human declined. Retroactive fabrication of rejection events requires knowing what the AI would have suggested — which requires having run the actual collaboration.

-----

### Property 3: Selection, Not Surrender

The human chose what survived. **The AI generated candidates; the human decided what was true to the work.**

Selection is the primary act of authorship under collaboration. Every kept sentence was a yes. Every discarded draft was a no. The accumulated pattern of yeses and nos is the author’s signature — more legible, in some ways, than the output itself.

*What makes this certifiable:* Session logs, draft archaeology, commit history — the artifacts of the selection process. The Quorum Time / Aegis Protocol provides tamper-evident timestamping for selection events, making the sequence forensically credible.

*What AI cannot fake retroactively:* Selection requires real-time judgment. The human brings context from outside the session — lived experience, values, awareness of what the work needs to be — that cannot be reconstructed from the session transcript alone.

-----

### Property 4: Thematic Control

Symbols, motifs, and arguments recur **because someone means them to recur** — not because the model is echoing its own embeddings from earlier in the session.

Thematic control is the most subtle of the five properties and the most revealing. AI produces thematic consistency through token prediction — patterns that cohere because they appeared together in training data. Human thematic control produces consistency through *intention* — the author returns to the same images because they matter, not because they co-occur statistically.

*What makes this certifiable:* The cross-domain contamination signature. When a work contains thematic material that derives from the author’s lived experience — knowledge the AI was never given, connections the AI could not have made from the prompts alone — the thematic control is demonstrably human. In the Taller Shell trilogy, this is present throughout: the marine noir world draws on sources the AI encountered only through the human’s direction, not through training data prompts.

*What AI cannot fake retroactively:* Cross-domain contamination requires the human to have existed with specific knowledge and experience. The connection between Black Forest clockmaking and a tidal fantasy world is not available to an AI unless the human brings it. That act of bringing is thematic control.

-----

### Property 5: Residue

The finished work feels like it was **wanted by someone**, not merely assembled. A reader can state what the author argued.

Residue is the phenomenological test — the reader’s experience of encountering a governing mind behind the work. It is the least technically certifiable of the five properties and the most important to articulate, because it is what readers and editors are actually testing when they ask “is this human?”

*What makes this certifiable:* The governing question (Sovereignty Paradox): *If the AI vanished, would the work still have had a soul, just less range?* The answer is demonstrated by identifying what the work is *about* — its argument, its emotional truth, its claim on the reader — and tracing that claim to a human source.

*What AI cannot fake retroactively:* Residue requires that someone *wanted* the work to exist for reasons that originated outside the work. AI generates content that satisfies prompts. Humans generate work that satisfies something in themselves. That distinction is detectable at the level of intention, even when it is not detectable at the level of style.

## The Three-Layer Attestation

Certification of the five properties operates through three layers of evidence. Each layer is independently valuable; together they constitute a chain that is structurally resistant to retroactive fabrication.

### Layer 1 — Process Evidence (The Decision Record)

**What it is:** A timestamped record of human decisions *about* AI output — not the AI’s output itself.

**Evidence nodes:**

- **Rejection events:** The human declined a suggestion the AI offered, with timestamp and brief rationale
- **Redirect events:** The human changed direction mid-session, introducing something the AI had not proposed
- **Constraint declarations:** The human imposed a rule for reasons outside the text (“I’m not including X because of Y”)
- **Session gaps:** Out-of-session continuation with documented direction change — the work evolved between sessions in ways that reflect biological time passing

**Process Provenance note:** As models train on increasing volumes of human-AI collaborative data, output-based tells compress — the artifact becomes less distinguishable from synthetic production regardless of how it was made. The process record is not subject to this compression. Time spent, revisions made, negative space explored, and decisions logged are not model artifacts; they are human artifacts. The durable provenance signal is in the decision record, not the text. This is why Layer 1 is the foundational layer and why its compression resistance increases as output-based detection degrades.

**The Quorum Time / Aegis Protocol connection:** Tamper-evident sequence numbers on each event make the process record forensically credible and SAFE_PAUSE auditable. The chain-of-custody model from the Trust Chain Protocol applies here: the human’s authorization chain is what the process record documents.

**Minimum viable version:** A simple log maintained by the author during collaboration — not forensic-grade cryptography, but consistent enough to demonstrate the pattern of governing decisions. Capturable on a phone. Lightweight enough not to interrupt the creative process.

-----

### Layer 2 — Origin Evidence (The Voice Fingerprint)

**What it is:** The dimensional voice signature that predates the AI collaboration and cannot be retroactively synthesized.

**Evidence nodes:**

- **ROSETTA baseline:** The pre-collaboration voice sample establishing what the author sounded like before AI assistance began
- **Cross-domain contamination:** Details in the work that derive from knowledge the AI was never given — the uninstructed bleed of lived experience
- **Idiosyncratic constraint:** Decisions that reflect the author’s values, biography, or experience — not craft choices, but person choices
- **Productive incoherence:** Contradictions or tensions the human preserved because they felt true, rather than resolving them as AI would

**Why this layer is structurally unfakeable:** Origin evidence requires the human to have *existed* in a particular way before the work began. Cross-domain contamination, biographical constraint, the specific incoherences a person lives with — these cannot be manufactured retroactively because they require being a specific person with a specific history.

**The AI tell inversion:** AI produces smooth option-space and optimized coherence. Human origin evidence includes orphaned decisions, productive contradiction, and knowledge that arrived through living rather than prompting. The absence of these in a work is itself a signal.

-----

### Layer 3 — Social Evidence (The External Anchor)

**What it is:** The forensic layer — evidence that exists outside the text and locates a specific sovereign person in relation to it.

**Evidence nodes:**

- Copyright registration timestamp (predating any challenge)
- Contemporaneous correspondence with named humans about the work
- Workshop, critique, or editorial records with identifiable participants
- The egoless-code corollary: documentation of human directorial oversight without authorial ego attached to specific outputs

**Why this layer matters:** Social evidence directly addresses the identity provenance vector from the Human Provenance Problem Map. It anchors the content to a specific person, not just a governing intelligence in the abstract. The chain-of-custody logic from the Trust Chain Protocol applies: trust must be traceable back to a human authorization anchor.

**The minimum viable version:** For an individual author, this is the copyright registration date and one piece of contemporaneous correspondence. Not institutional infrastructure — one verifiable external anchor that predates any challenge.

## The Collaboration State Spectrum

Standard provenance thinking is binary: human or AI. The HSCF introduces a second axis — the collaboration modality.

```
← AI-only ←————→ AI-led ←————→ Co-equal ←————→ Human-led ←————→ Human-only →
                                                     ↑
                                           Target certification zone
                                           ("human-led with AI assistance")
                                           Empirically located by Tell Engine v2.1
                                           on The Taller Shell Trilogy
```

Works legitimately occupy different positions on this spectrum. The HSCF certifies *where* a work sits and *why* — not as a verdict but as a modality description. “Human-led with AI assistance” is not a compromise or a hedge. It is a specific, positive state that the ecosystem was built to demonstrate is both achievable and verifiable.

The detector that flagged the Taller Shell collaboration as “human-led with AI assistance” was not wrong. It correctly identified the collaboration modality. The author stopped trying to defeat the detector when he recognized this — because the detected state was the goal state. The HSCF formalizes that recognition.

## The Egoless Work Dimension

One design constraint that this framework must honor — documented from empirical practice:

The entire ecosystem documented in this framework was built by one person, without a technical background, working on a mobile device. That is not a biographical footnote. It is a proof of concept.

It demonstrates that:

1. **Capability is no longer the gate.** Human sovereign collaboration enables individuals to build, write, research, and create at scales that previously required teams. The one-person-on-mobile fact is evidence about what becomes possible when the human governs and the AI amplifies.
1. **Egoless work is a collaboration property, not a personality trait.** Code and prose produced under human sovereign collaboration tend to have no ego investment in specific outputs — the human’s identity is attached to the *governing intention*, not the *execution artifacts*. This makes the work more robust (it can be criticized without threatening the author’s identity) and more legible as human-governed (the governance decisions are visible precisely because the execution isn’t defended).
1. **The framework must be accessible.** A certification instrument that requires cryptographic infrastructure, institutional backing, or technical expertise fails on its own terms. The minimum viable provenance signal must be capturable on a phone by someone with no tech background. The Safe Storyteller and Addendum B principles apply here: sovereignty preservation for the people who need it most, not just the people with the most resources.

## Structural Tells That May Be Permanent

The Human Provenance Problem Map estimates the stylometric detection window at 4–6 months for casual detection. This is probably accurate for surface tells.

But some tells may be structurally permanent — not artifacts of current AI limitations, but properties of *what AI is*:

|Structural AI Property                |Why It’s Unfakeable                                                                  |Human Counterpart                                                                     |
|--------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Smooth option-space**               |AI considers all paths before outputting; produces no path-dependence artifacts      |Human drafts show path-dependence — you can’t un-know what you wrote in chapter two   |
|**No orphaned decisions**             |AI output has no artifacts of abandoned reasoning                                    |Humans leave traces of what they almost did                                           |
|**Optimized coherence**               |AI prose has higher internal semantic consistency than human prose across long works |Humans contradict themselves across a long work in ways that are narratively authentic|
|**Absence of the unplanned**          |AI can simulate surprise but cannot have it                                          |Human-authored work contains texture shifts that follow unplanned developments        |
|**Training-domain gravity**           |AI novelty is recombinant; it pulls toward training distribution at statistical scale|Structural originality — form invented for this specific work — is a human signal     |
|**No uninstructed cross-domain bleed**|AI only knows what it was given; it cannot import knowledge from a life              |The Taller Shell trilogy contains details traceable to sources never in any prompt    |

The 4–6 month window is for the surface. These structural properties are not stylometric — they are architectural. They derive from the nature of token prediction, not from current model limitations. As models improve, they may become better at *simulating* these properties, but simulation of path-dependence is not path-dependence. The HSCF’s Layer 2 (origin evidence) is precisely the layer that exposes the simulation.

This is the publishable claim: **some human tells are permanent because they require being a specific person with a specific history, and AI cannot retroactively acquire a history.**

## What This Framework Does Not Cover

Applying the Negative Space Mapper:

**OVERLOOKED — Consent scope.** The HSCF certifies what was done; it does not yet address what was authorized. Addendum B’s posthumous ethics framework points toward the architecture needed here: human sovereign governance requires not just that a human was deciding, but that the human was aware of and consented to what the AI was contributing. Consent scope is a gap in v0.1.

**OVERLOOKED — Real-time voice provenance.** The Human Provenance Problem Map correctly identifies voice as a structurally different problem. The HSCF covers documents. A live voice interaction has no artifact to anchor provenance evidence to at the moment of production. This is a separate instrument.

**OVERLOOKED — False negative protection.** The Sovereignty Paradox documents that high-quality human-AI collaboration is already being misread as synthetic. The HSCF v0.1 addresses certification for use cases where the author initiates the claim. It does not yet address the case where an author needs to *defend* work that has been challenged. The adversarial use case requires additional architecture.

**STRUCTURAL — The next document.** The Human Provenance Problem Map promises a trust model mapping: who needs what verified, under what conditions, with what consequences for failure. That mapping is the necessary precursor to any technical implementation of the HSCF. It is not in this document.

**DELIBERATE — Technical implementation.** The attestation layers are characterized here. Their technical implementation — how the process log is stored, how origin evidence is preserved, what constitutes a valid social anchor in different institutional contexts — is intentionally deferred. Get the framework right first.

## Safe Storyteller and Addendum B: The Constitutive Case

Appendix A (Safe Storyteller) and Appendix B (Emotional Safety Protocols) are the clearest proof-of-concept for the HSCF’s central claim — and not in the way one might expect.

Both documents define conditions under which human presence is not just verifiable but *constitutive*. The hospitalized child’s story exists to give the child agency back. An AI that fully owned the authorship would defeat the purpose of the tool. The grieving user’s authentic memory of the person they lost is what heals — an idealized AI reconstruction harms, regardless of quality.

In both cases, **removing human sovereignty doesn’t produce a lesser version of the same thing — it produces a different and harmful thing**. This is the governing question applied in reverse: *if the human vanished, would the work still do what it was supposed to do?* In these contexts, no. The human presence is not an optional quality upgrade. It is what the work *is*.

This is the theoretical foundation of the HSCF that goes beyond authorship credit: human sovereign governance is sometimes not about attribution. It is about whether the thing being created can exist at all in the way it needs to.
