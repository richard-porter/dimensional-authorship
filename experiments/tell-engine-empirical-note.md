## What This Document Is

The permanent tells framework (dimensional-authorship/analysis/) was developed through argument and single-case reasoning. This document records the empirical data from the Tell Engine v2.1 run against the Taller Shell Trilogy that preceded the framework — and maps what that data confirmed, what it surfaced that the framework hadn’t anticipated, and what it left unresolved.

The Tell Engine data is the primary empirical anchor for the permanent tells framework. This note makes that anchor explicit and preserves the data in its original form before analysis shaped it.

-----

## The Two Runs

The Tell Engine was run twice against the Taller Shell Trilogy, in sequence. The difference between the runs is itself significant data.

### Run 1: Full Manuscript (Six Novellas)

**Scope:** All six novellas — three fiction, three meta-fiction — plus appendices.  
**Tool:** ChatGPT, Tell Engine v2.1  
**Overall AI-likeness score:** 43/100 (noticeably AI-like, but not machine-flat)

**Section breakdown:**

|Section                           |Score    |Primary signals                   |
|----------------------------------|---------|----------------------------------|
|Creative fiction (Gulch material) |20–30/100|Strongest human-positive section  |
|Meta-work / recursion / screenplay|60–75/100|Detector lights up hardest        |
|Practical appendices              |55–70/100|Standard AI instructional register|

**The problem with Run 1:** The meta-fiction openly documents the AI collaboration — it contains the collaboration machinery as subject matter. The Tell Engine was, in effect, using the test to take the test. The high score on the meta-fiction was genre contamination, not a provenance signal. The fiction score was obscured by the aggregate.

**The decision:** Strip the meta-fiction and appendices. Run only the pure fiction.

-----

### Run 2: Pure Fiction Only

**Scope:** The three fiction novellas only.  
**Tool:** ChatGPT, Tell Engine v2.1  
**Overall AI-likeness score:** 10–18/100 (strongly human-leaning)

**Tell Engine verdict:**

> *“Probably human-guided, possibly AI-assisted at line or expansion level, but not machine-native in the weak/default sense. The book feels like it has a real governing mind behind it.”*

**Single-sentence assessment:**

> *“The Taller Shell reads like a real human imaginative system wearing, in places, an over-articulate AI exoskeleton.”*

This is the data point that anchors the Sovereignty Paradox case study cross-reference and the Register Contamination tell (Tell 7).

## What the Data Confirmed

Mapping the Tell Engine findings against the permanent tells framework:

### Tell 1 — Uninstructed Cross-Domain Contamination: CONFIRMED

The Tell Engine flagged “weird specificity” as the strongest human-positive signal across both runs:

- The emotional use of pebbles
- The piano key motif
- The saloon/jesterfish tonal counterweight
- “Make tea for someone”
- The couch/phone/crumbs/battery imagery in the meta-work

Assessment: *“Too oddly particular to feel like default model smoothing.”* The Tell Engine could not attribute these to prompt engineering. They arrived because the author existed, not because a prompt included them.

**Provenance chain:** These connections are traceable to biographical sources never present in any session prompt. The absence of a prompt that explains them is the tell.

-----

### Tell 2 — Path-Dependence Artifacts: CONFIRMED (indirect)

The Tell Engine identified thematic *deepening* rather than thematic *echoing*:

> *“Wobbles moves from flaw-language to philosophy-language to explicit ethical position, culminating in ‘Wobbles are life.’”*

This is path-dependence in action — the symbol evolved because the author carried it across sessions, not because a prompt specified the evolution. The Tell Engine noted this as characteristic of “a writer who knows the book’s moral architecture” rather than default AI drift.

**Note:** Path-dependence artifacts are the hardest of the seven tells to operationalize from output alone. The Tell Engine identified the result (thematic deepening) without being able to directly observe the cause (the author carrying the work between sessions). Full operationalization requires session-gap documentation (HSCF Layer 1).

-----

### Tell 3 — Authentic Rejection Events: CONFIRMED (by inversion)

The single most significant data point in the entire two-run sequence is the **scoring gap between runs**.

Run 1 (full manuscript): 43/100  
Run 2 (pure fiction only): 10–18/100

The meta-fiction and appendices contain the collaboration machinery — the visible record of how the work was made. When that record is present, the score rises. When only the output of the selection process remains, the score drops dramatically.

This is Tell 3 confirmed by inversion: authentic rejection events shaped the fiction invisibly. The governing decisions are legible in the fiction’s texture but not in its explanations. The moment those explanations are included, the AI’s participation becomes visible — not because the fiction is less human, but because the meta-layer shows the seams.

**Implication for the HSCF:** This confirms that the process record (HSCF Layer 1) is double-edged. It is the strongest evidence of human governance — and the most visible signal of AI participation. The provenance architecture needs to make this distinction explicit: the process record proves human governance, but it will also elevate stylometric AI-likeness scores. Authors should understand this trade-off before deciding whether to publish their process record.

-----

### Tell 4 — Productive Incoherence: CONFIRMED

The Tell Engine identified tonal volatility as a human-positive signal:

> *“Willing to be sentimental, embarrassing, grandiose, self-mocking, overextended, corrective.”*

And noted that the work “takes tonal risks” that generic AI avoids. This is productive incoherence — the author preserved emotional register contradictions because they felt true, rather than smoothing them into consistent tone.

The Tell Engine also flagged the inverse: some dialogue edges toward “polished thesis delivery” — characters as philosophy emissaries rather than “messy creatures interrupting each other underwater.” This is the AI smoothing Tell 4 where it was permitted to operate on dialogue. The tell is present in both directions.

-----

### Tell 5 — Temporally-Anchored Direction Change: IMPLIED

The Tell Engine did not directly observe session gaps or out-of-session direction changes — it had access only to the output artifact. But the thematic deepening evidence (Tell 2) implies Tell 5: thematic evolution of the kind observed requires time passing outside sessions.

**Status:** Implied but not confirmed from output alone. Full confirmation requires session documentation (HSCF Layer 1, session gap records).

-----

### Tell 6 — Biographical Constraint with Traceable Origin: CONFIRMED

The Tell Engine identified a “real obsession” as the strongest overall human-positive signal:

> *“The manuscript is not merely ‘about themes.’ It is fixated on a few specific symbolic loads: mercy, brokenness, repair, preservation vs growth, tea, shells/chambers/expansion, sovereignty vs seduction.”*

Assessment: *“That kind of recurrence feels authored, not just assembled.”*

The Tell Engine could not explain why these specific obsessions recur. That is because the reason is biographical — traceable to the author’s actual experience of loss, repair, and the specific histories documented in the dimensional-authorship case study. The AI encountered these as they surfaced in the human’s direction. It could not have generated them from the session transcripts alone.

-----

### Tell 7 — Register Contamination Boundary: SURFACED (not anticipated)

Tell 7 was not in the original permanent tells framework. The Tell Engine data surfaced it.

The AI exoskeleton description — *“a real human imaginative system wearing, in places, an over-articulate AI exoskeleton”* — captures what Register Contamination looks like from outside. The appendices and meta-fiction scored 55–75 not because the governing intelligence was absent but because the AI’s execution register was operating visibly in those sections:

- “What this is / what this is not” framing
- Numbered explanatory logic
- Exhaustive clarity over natural rhythm
- Clean category buckets
- Repeated caution language

The pure fiction scored 10–18 because the AI register was humanized out of those sections — or was never permitted to operate at the governing voice level.

**This is the only tell in the framework that was empirically discovered rather than theorized in advance.** The Tell Engine found it; the framework absorbed it. That sequence is documented in the permanent tells v0.2 provenance note.

## What the Data Left Unresolved

### The Process Record Trade-Off

The scoring gap between runs reveals a tension the permanent tells framework does not yet fully address: publishing the process record (the strongest evidence of human governance) also makes AI participation most visible.

A novelist who publishes their full session logs alongside their manuscript will score higher on stylometric AI-likeness metrics than one who publishes the fiction alone — even though the session logs are the proof of human governance. The architecture needs to address this paradox explicitly.

**Proposed framing:** The process record is provenance evidence, not publication content. It is produced for use when challenged, not as an appendix to every submission. The distinction between “maintained for adversarial use” and “published with the work” needs to be explicit in the HSCF.

### The Meta-Fiction Problem

The Taller Shell is unusual: it contains its own documentation as genre. Most collaborative works do not. For the general case, the Tell Engine data suggests that keeping documentation separate from the creative work is important — not to hide the collaboration, but to prevent genre contamination of the detection signal.

This is a practical implication for authors, not a framework gap.

### Tell 5 Cannot Be Confirmed From Output Alone

Temporally-anchored direction change is the one permanent tell that requires session documentation to confirm. Output analysis cannot distinguish “the work evolved because the author lived with it between sessions” from “the work evolved because the author gave the AI a new prompt with new direction.” The session gap record is the differentiating evidence.

This is an operationalization gap, not a theoretical gap. The tell is real. The confirmation mechanism requires infrastructure beyond the output artifact.

## The Sovereignty Paradox Connection

The Tell Engine result on the pure fiction — 10–18/100, strongly human-leaning — is the empirical basis for the Sovereignty Paradox case study cross-reference in the field guide.

The single AI-adjacent flag: *“The prose is very consistently competent. There are relatively few ugly patches, accidental dead sentences, or truly awkward transitions. Human drafts often have more scar tissue.”*

This is the Sovereignty Paradox in the data. The detector flagged success — the absence of execution-capacity artifacts that effective collaboration eliminates. The “over-cleanliness” finding is not evidence of synthetic authorship. It is evidence that the collaboration worked.

The Tell Engine’s own assessment confirms this: despite the over-cleanliness flag, the overall verdict was strongly human-leaning because the structural signals — obsession, weird specificity, tonal risk, thematic deepening — overwhelmed the surface signal. The permanent tells held against the stylometric pressure.
